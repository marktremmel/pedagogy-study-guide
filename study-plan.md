3. **Review the glossary**
   - Read through all terms once
   - Identify which ones are completely new vs. familiar

### **Pass 2: Active Recall (Days 3-4)**
**Goal**: Test yourself and identify weak spots

1. **Use the interactive flashcard app** (see `index.html`)
   - Study in 25-minute sessions (Pomodoro technique)
   - Focus on terms you struggle with
   - Mark cards as "known" or "review again"

2. **Practice the short-answer quiz** (without looking at answers)
   - Write out answers in your own words
   - Compare with the answer key
   - Note which questions you couldn't answer fully

3. **Teach-back method**
   - Explain each concept out loud as if teaching someone
   - Record yourself or explain to a friend/rubber duck
   - If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet

### **Pass 3: Deep Synthesis (Days 5-7)**
**Goal**: Connect concepts and prepare for essay questions

1. **Create comparison tables**
   - Example: Parenting styles and their outcomes
   - Example: Digital Natives vs. Digital Immigrants
   - Example: Primary vs. Secondary groups

2. **Practice essay outlines**
   - For each of the 5 essay questions, create a brief outline
   - Identify which concepts from the glossary relate to each question
   - Find connections between different topics

3. **Final self-test**
   - Complete the short-answer quiz again (timed: 30 minutes)
   - Write at least 2 full essay responses
   - Review and refine your answers

## ðŸ’¡ Memory Techniques

### **Acronyms & Mnemonics**

**FSANP** - Group Development Phases:
- **F**orming
- **S**torming (conflicts!)
- **N**orming (rules established)
- **P**erforming (we-consciousness)
- **A**djourning (goodbye)

**AAP** - Parenting Styles:
- **A**uthoritarian (punishment, obedient kids)
- **A**uthoritative (explanation, responsible kids)
- **P**ermissive (no limits, immature kids)

**The CULTURE House**:
- **C**ulture is the landscape (foundation)
- **E**nculturation teaches you the cultural basics (materials)
- **S**ocialization is the blueprint (structure)
- **E**ducation is the construction work (deliberate)
- **I**ndividualization is the unique finished home (you)

### **Story-Based Memory**

**The Digital Divide Story**:
> Imagine two people at a computer. The **Digital Immigrant** reads the manual, takes notes, does one task at a time. The **Digital Native** just... knows. They're texting, gaming, and watching videos simultaneously, frustrated when WiFi takes 3 seconds. The Immigrant learned the language; the Native was born speaking it.

**The Mean World Syndrome Story**:
> A child watches 100 action movies. They start to think: "The world has explosions on every corner! Everyone is dangerous!" They're not aggressive themselvesâ€”they're **afraid**. The world seems "mean" and scary, even though their peaceful neighborhood hasn't changed.
